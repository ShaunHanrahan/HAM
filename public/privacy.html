🛰 Privacy & data

How HAM handles your data · last updated 28 June 2026

HAM is a satellite tracker. It runs almost entirely in your browser. There are no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party tracking. This page explains the little data it does use.

What's stored on your device

HAM uses your browser's local storage (similar to cookies, but it never leaves your browser and is never sent to our server):

  • Orbit cache (tle:<id>) — orbital elements for satellites you view, kept for ~12 hours. This is strictly necessary: it lets the app animate satellites without repeatedly calling the rate-limited orbit API, so it is always on.
  • Your preferences (ham:settings) — the filters and view options you choose (hidden constellations, category, sky radius, camera-lock and orbit/ground-track toggles, and your reminder lead time). Stored only if you click “Accept”.
  • Your favorites (ham:favorites) — the satellites you star, and whether each has a pass reminder turned on (just the satellite's catalogue number, name, and an on/off flag). Stored only if you click “Accept”.
  • Your choice (ham:consent) — whether you accepted or declined preference storage, so you aren't asked again.

No accounts, names, emails, or identifiers are stored. None of the above is transmitted to or stored on the HAM server.

Your location

If you allow it (via your browser's own permission prompt), HAM reads your approximate location to centre the "satellites overhead" view on you. Your coordinates are sent to the n2yo API (through this site's server, which only forwards the request) to find which satellites are above you. Your coordinates are not stored — by the app, or on the server. You can decline the browser prompt and enter coordinates manually (or use 0°, 0°) instead.

Notifications & reminders

You can star satellites as favorites and switch on a pass reminder for any of them. When you do:

  • HAM asks your browser for notification permission only when you turn on your first reminder — never before. If you decline, the reminder simply stays off.
  • The time a satellite will next rise over you is calculated entirely on your device from its orbital elements. Your favorites and reminder settings are never sent to the HAM server or anywhere else.
  • Reminders are shown using your browser's own notifications and only fire while HAM is open in a browser tab (including a background tab). HAM has no background push service, so nothing runs when the tab is closed.

Third-party services

To work, the app requests data from these services as needed (satellite IDs/names, and your coordinates for the overhead query):

  • n2yo — satellite positions and orbital elements.
  • SatNOGS DB — transmitter frequencies.
  • Wikimedia / Wikipedia — satellite descriptions and images.

These services have their own privacy policies. Satellite images shown in the "About this satellite" popup are loaded directly from Wikimedia.
